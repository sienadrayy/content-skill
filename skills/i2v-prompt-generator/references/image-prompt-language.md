# Image Prompt Language Reference

Vocabulary and patterns for writing detailed, sensual image prompts that work perfectly with i2v models and siena LoRA.

## Structure Template

Every image prompt follows this structure:

```
[Composition] of siena [in/wearing/with...] [clothing], [body position/pose], [facial expression/eyes], [background], [lighting], [style/quality]
```

## Subject & Framing

### Framing Types
- **Close-up portrait** - Face and upper shoulders
- **Close-up face** - Extreme face focus
- **Medium shot** - Head to waist
- **Full body** - Head to toe
- **Overhead shot** - Looking down at subject
- **Side profile** - 90° angle to camera
- **45° angle** - Three-quarter view (most flattering)

### Always Include
- **"siena"** - Always name her directly
- **Clothing description** - Specific (silk, mesh, minimal, etc.)
- **Body position** - Exact pose/positioning

## Pose & Body Positioning

### Seated Poses
- **Seated recline** - Leaning back, open chest
- **Seated on edge** - Perched, legs positioned
- **Cross-legged confident** - Grounded power pose
- **Lounging** - Relaxed but posed, usually on side
- **Kneeling** - Vulnerable but strong positioning
- **Lap/lap recline** - Lying back on surface

### Standing Poses
- **S-curve stand** - Hip out, chest open (curves emphasized)
- **Confident stance** - Shoulders back, grounded
- **Leaning pose** - Casual lean against surface
- **Over-shoulder stance** - Looking back, shoulders prominent
- **Walking** - Slow, intentional movement captured

### Angle Descriptors
- **Shoulder back to camera** - Emphasizes back and shoulders
- **Chest open to camera** - Face and upper body exposure
- **Side profile** - Shows silhouette
- **Lying on side** - Full side body visible
- **Angled away** - Suggestive positioning
- **Direct to camera** - Full frontal confidence

## Clothing & Materials

## Best Practices for i2v Models

1. **Be specific about pose** - i2v models respond well to exact positioning
2. **Include material details** - "Silk" vs "cotton" makes visual difference
3. **Lighting is critical** - Most sensual content needs warm, directional light
4. **Always name siena** - LoRA model works best with consistent naming
5. **Avoid negative descriptors** - Focus on what you want, not what to avoid
6. **Be sensual, not explicit** - Descriptive and engaging, not crude
7. **Include style/quality** - Helps model understand the aesthetic tier
